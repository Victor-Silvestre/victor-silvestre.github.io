My Journey

2008

I grew up in a small, family-owned photography studio, helping out as an assistant photographer and getting hands-on experience with Adobe Photoshop and video editing early on. By 16, I was creating videos and artwork for my local team, one of which got printed for a tifo, and they played some of my videos during the play-offs. Not sure if it had much impact, but hey, the team got promoted, so I’ll call it a win.

At home, my grandma was always messing with the TV remote, so I became the go-to person for fixing whatever she broke. Navigating Czechoslovak menus and getting things running probably marked my first dive into IT.

2011-2015

As I study Advertising & Public Relations in university, I apply the gained knowledge to the family business. I work on the corporate image, create a new website or boost alliances with other companies.

I take the graduation photographies that my parents had been doing for my class during my school years to a whole new level. I create a network to reach all possible schools, high schools and university degrees. I create batch automations to fine tune the pictures taken and templates with Adobe Indesign to have good results in little time.

2016

I finish my career doing Erasmus in Southampton, UK. My scholarship arrives 5 months later than expected. I survive on savings of the graduation photography project, working in a local theme park as a ride operator, playing semi pro football and doing some video editing and graphic design gigs for people that I met there. I consolidate my English knowledge and learn to be comfortable by being uncomfortable.

2017-2019

Love takes me to Italy. There I start working in a technologic start up that produces large format printers. My role says "product technician". My bosses call me "SuperVictor".

"SuperVictor" does user interfaces for all the products, file optimization for large format printing, color management, web design, collateral, product manuals, video maintenance support, interactive design for display screens and graphics for trade fairs. It comes available in 4 languages and can perform product demonstrations and staff training in the destination where the product is installed.

My supervisor is a Software Developer. From him, I learn to think as one, and I start to become familiar with coding.

2020-2021

In Barcelona, I help a friend to start up a business about goalkeeper gloves. I create the brand and the website based on Wordpress and Woocommerce. The business grows up quick, adapting to new demands and expanding to international markets. The digital infrastructure established holds up so well that it becomes a model to apply to other entrepeneurships.

2022

I work for a digital marketing agency in the center of Barcelona. I get the chance to work in tens of different projects, most of them in Wordpress environments but also some Shopify stores. The drill is: I get the designs from a very talented graphic designer, I implement them, and present it to the clients.

2023-2025

Freelance time! I am putting all the skills I’ve learned over the years to the service of the most interesting entrepreneurships and businesses. From designing WordPress websites to crafting custom digital solutions, I focus on helping small businesses and startups establish or enhance their online presence.

My approach combines creativity, functionality, and business strategy. I collaborate closely with my clients to ensure their brands not only look great but also operate efficiently and align with their goals. Whether it’s building robust e-commerce platforms, optimizing user experiences, or providing ongoing advice, I strive to be a reliable partner in their growth journey.
