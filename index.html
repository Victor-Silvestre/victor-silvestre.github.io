My Journey

2008

I grew up in a small, family-owned photography studio.

Helping out as an assistant photographer and getting hands-on experience with Adobe Photoshop and video editing was my first work experience. By 15, I was creating videos and artwork for the football local team that I supported, one of which got printed for a tifo, and they played some of my videos to the team during the play-offs. Not sure if it had much impact, but hey, the team got promoted, so I’ll call it a win.

At home, my grandma was always messing with the TV remote, so I became the go-to person for fixing whatever she broke. Navigating Czechoslovak menus and getting things running probably marked my first dive into IT.

2011-2015

I study Advertising & Public Relations in university, specializing in graphic design.

I apply the gained knowledge to the family business. I work on the corporate image, create a new website or boost alliances with other companies. I took the graduation photography services that my parents had been offering for my school class to a whole new level. I created a commercial network to reach schools, high schools, and universities in my region, and developed batch automations to fine-tune the pictures. I also used Adobe InDesign to create efficient templates, ensuring high-quality results in less time.

2016

Erasmus in Southampton, UK.

I finished my university degree here taking subjects from the "BBA, Computer Administration Sciences and Support Services".

My scholarship arrived 5 months later than expected, so I survived on savings from my graduation photography project, working in a local theme park as a ride operator, playing semi-pro football, and doing video editing and graphic design gigs for people I met there. I strengthened my English fluency and learned the art of being comfortable with discomfort.

2017-2019

I work in a technologic start up near Milano, Italy.

Love takes me to Italy, where I secure a position in this company (NoeCha SRL) that produces advanced large format printers. They needed someone with graphic design skills to prepare or fix files for large format printing and that could also speak languages to perform product demonstrations in the European market.

My role says "product technician." but my bosses end up calling me "SuperVictor".

"SuperVictor" understands the product by operating it and has a vision about how it is perceived by the public by observing other operators and client experiences. The technology and engineering is great, but the visual identity does not reflect that and the means to use the machines are not so user-friendly. That's how he took the intiative and designed the new user interfaces for all the products, created a new website, collateral, product manuals, video maintenance support, Youtube channel, Twitter account, interactive design for display screens in trade fairs, etc.

He is also available in four languages and can perform product demonstrations and staff training in the destination where the product is installed.

Moreover, he can also act as a proxy mechanical engineer, software engineer or electrician if things got sideways far from home.

My supervisor is a very talented Software Developer. From him, I learn to think as one, and I start to become familiar with coding.

2020-2021

I help a friend to start up a business in Barcelona, Spain.

My main role consists in creating the brand and the website based on Wordpress and Woocommerce.

The business (TwoFive Gloves) is about goalkeeper gloves (goalkeeping is part of my life!) and it grows up quick, adapting to new demands and expanding to international markets. The digital infrastructure established holds up so well that it becomes a model to apply to other entrepeneurships.

During the begining of this period I work briefly for Meypar (Maquinaria Estacionamientos y Parkímetros, S.L.) as Digital Consultant but the contract was cut short due to COVID-19 pandemic. I caused a good impression and eventually they wanted to restart my contract, but I decide to dedicate myself fully to TwoFive due to its exponential growth.

2022

I work in a digital marketing agency as a front-end developer. I get the chance to work in tens of different projects.

Most of them in Wordpress environments but also some Shopify stores. The drill is: I get the designs from a very talented graphic designer, I implement them, and present the finished digital project to the clients.

Here's where I meet Akrovalis SL, who enventually requires me specifically for their projects.

2023-2025

Freelance time! I am putting all the skills I’ve learned over the years to the service of the most interesting entrepreneurships and businesses. From designing WordPress websites to crafting custom digital solutions, I focus on helping small businesses and startups establish or enhance their online presence.

My approach combines creativity, functionality, and business strategy. I collaborate closely with my clients to ensure their brands not only look great but also operate efficiently and align with their goals. Whether it’s building robust e-commerce platforms, optimizing user experiences, or providing ongoing advice, I strive to be a reliable partner in their growth journey.
